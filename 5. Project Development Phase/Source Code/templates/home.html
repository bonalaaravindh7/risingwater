Introduction

Understanding flood forecasting

Flood forecasting uses historical rainfall and weather data to estimate flood risk ahead of time, giving communities a lead time that can range from a few hours to several days. It's a core part of flood warning systems, where forecasting focuses on predicting what will happen while warning focuses on turning that prediction into a decision. This tool applies a machine-learning model trained on regional rainfall and climate records to give you a fast, early read on flood risk from a handful of local inputs.
